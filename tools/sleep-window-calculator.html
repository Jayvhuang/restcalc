How the sleep window calculator works

A sleep window calculator starts from the hours you are free to sleep — say 10 PM to 7 AM — rather than a fixed bedtime. It works forward in 15-minute steps, keeping only the bedtimes whose full 90-minute cycles end inside your window. The result is a list of bedtimes that fit your real schedule and still complete whole cycles.

If your evenings are unpredictable, this is more useful than a single bedtime. You see every option that lands you on a cycle boundary and wakes you inside your window — so you can pick the one that suits the night.

Why plan around a window?

Real life is not a fixed clock. A window respects your constraints (work, childcare, shift patterns) while still protecting sleep quality by completing full cycles. The 90-minute model here follows sleep science such as the National Sleep Foundation's duration guidance (2022). Most adults do best with 4–6 cycles; the helper tells you how many your window can hold.

A few quick tips

  • Make the window at least 6–9 hours for an adult.
  • Earlier bedtimes inside the window give more margin.
  • Compare with the Bedtime and Wake-up tools for a single target.
