How the sleep log works

A sleep log turns a handful of nights into something you can actually see. Enter your bedtime, wake time and how you felt for each night, and the tracker works out your total sleep, your completed 90-minute cycles, and a regularity score — then draws it as a trend.

One night tells you almost nothing. A week tells you whether you are short on sleep, whether your bedtimes wander, and how often you wake refreshed. That pattern is where the useful changes live.

Privacy by design

Everything stays in your own browser. Your entries are saved locally (localStorage) and are never uploaded to us or any server. Clear your browser data and they are gone. This is the same private-by-design approach used across every Restcalc tool.

A few quick tips

  • Log at least 5–7 nights to see a real trend.
  • Keep bedtimes consistent — regularity matters as much as total hours.
  • Use the Bedtime and Wake-up tools to plan the next night.

Frequently asked questions about the Sleep Log & Trend

Everything you need to track your sleep confidently.

What does the sleep log track?▾
It records your bedtime, wake time and how you felt for several nights, then shows average sleep, average cycles, regularity and refreshed mornings as a trend.
Is my sleep data private?▾
Yes. Entries are saved only in your own browser (localStorage). They are never uploaded to us or any server, and clearing your browser data erases them.
How are cycles calculated in the log?▾
Each night uses the 90-minute model: time in bed minus ~15 minutes to fall asleep, divided by 90. It is an estimate, not measured sleep.
Why track sleep trends?▾
A few nights of data reveal patterns — short sleep, irregular bedtimes, or low refreshed mornings — that a single night hides. Small, consistent changes add up.
