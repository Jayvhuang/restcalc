How the nap calculator works

A nap calculator takes the current time and adds your natural 90-minute sleep cycle — plus about 15 minutes to fall asleep — to show two useful targets: a 20-minute power nap and a 90-minute full-cycle nap. Both avoid deep sleep, so you wake clear instead of groggy.

If you have asked "when should I nap?", the short answer is early afternoon. A nap that lands you in deep sleep, or one taken late in the day, steals from the night. This tool keeps you in the safe window.

Power nap vs full-cycle nap

A 20-minute power nap is a quick reset — boosted alertness with no sleep inertia. A 90-minute nap lets you complete one full cycle and wake at a light-sleep boundary, which feels more like genuine rest. Pick the one your schedule allows, and keep it before about 3 PM.

A few quick tips

  • Nap before 3 PM to protect bedtime.
  • Keep it under 90 minutes unless you can full-cycle.
  • Use a dark, cool space so you actually fall asleep.

Frequently asked questions about the Nap Calculator

Everything you need to nap smart.

What is the best length for a nap?▾
A 20-minute power nap boosts alertness without deep sleep, while a 90-minute full-cycle nap lets you complete one cycle and wake refreshed.
When should I take a nap?▾
Early afternoon is best. Keep naps before about 3 PM and under 90 minutes so they do not steal from nighttime sleep.
Why avoid long late naps?▾
Long or late naps can push your bedtime later and reduce deep sleep at night. A short, early nap protects your regular schedule.
Can I use this for shift work?▾
Yes. Enter your current time and the calculator shows the best window for a refreshing nap within your break.
